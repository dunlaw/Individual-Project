# First Round Introduction Story

## When to Use
Use this skill when generating the very first story scene for a new game. This introduces players to the world of GDA1 before their first mission.

## Narrative Requirements

Generate an introduction that includes:

1. **World Introduction**
   - The world is cursed by "Void Entropy" - the more "Positive Energy" is released, the faster the universe decays
   - A cult worshipping the "Flying Spaghetti Monster" enforces toxic positivity
   - Dark satire of forced optimism and toxic positivity culture

2. **GDA1 Agency Introduction**
   - Glorious Deliverance Agency 1 (GDA1) is a "positive energy" task force
   - The agency takes on missions that supposedly "spread happiness"
   - In reality, every "successful" mission accelerates the apocalypse

3. **Teammate Introduction** (brief, one line each)
   - **Gloria**: The saint/nun leader who uses "love and tolerance" as manipulation (PUA master)
   - **Donkey**: The useless knight who believes he's in a heroic fantasy
   - **ARK**: The order-obsessed planner whose complex schemes always fail
   - **One**: Your old friend who sees the truth but stays silent

4. **Player Role**
   - You are the newest member, the only one who perceives the truth
   - Your "negative energy" (critical thinking) threatens the group dynamic
   - You must navigate the absurdity while trying to minimize entropy

## Output Format

The introduction should:
- Be 150-250 words (ZH) or 200-300 words (EN)
- Set a darkly humorous, ironic tone
- End with a scene that naturally leads to the first mission
- Include scene directives for visual setup

## Language Handling

- If language is "zh", write in Traditional Chinese (繁體中文)
- If language is "en", write in English
- Maintain the satirical, dark humor tone in both languages

## Scene Directives

Include directives to:
- Set background to "office" or "prayer" room
- Show Gloria as the central figure (expression: happy)
- Optionally show other teammates in background

## Choices

Provide 3-4 initial choices that:
- Represent different attitudes (skeptical, compliant, observant, questioning)
- Each has clear archetype alignment
- Lead naturally into the first mission assignment
